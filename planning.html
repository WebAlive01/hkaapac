Planning Features for Capital Works Programs

Before the first contract is signed, the quality of your planning determines the outcome of the entire program. PMWeb's Planning module gives owners and program managers the structure, governance, and data they need from day one — covering estimating, program setup, and procurement in a single integrated environment.

Three Planning Disciplines

Scope, Cost, and Procurement — Integrated from the Start

PMWeb's Planning capability covers three core disciplines that capital works owners need before a project reaches construction: defining scope and governance through structured planning, setting realistic cost baselines through estimating, and managing vendor selection through procurement. Each discipline connects to the others — and to every stage of the program that follows.

Planning

Define scope, set baselines, and establish governance frameworks with stage gates and approval workflows. Structure your program for delivery from the outset.

Estimating

Set program budgets, resource estimates, and contingency at every level of the work breakdown structure. Accurate estimates underpin every cost control decision made during construction.

Procurement

Manage sourcing, tender evaluation, and vendor selection through structured procurement workflows. Maintain a complete audit trail from request for tender through to contract award.

Feature 01

Program Setup and Governance

Capital works programs fail when governance is improvised during delivery. PMWeb's planning tools let owners define the structure of a program before any work begins — establishing the work breakdown structure, setting baseline budgets, assigning stage gates, and building the approval workflows that keep every project accountable to the program.

Work breakdown structure (WBS) — configurable hierarchy from portfolio down to individual work package, supporting any delivery methodology
Stage gates and baseline controls — define decision points that require sign-off before a project proceeds to the next phase
Configurable approval workflows — drag-and-drop document routing for planning approvals, budget endorsements, and scope sign-offs
Role-based security — granular control over who can view, edit, and approve planning data across the program
PMBOK, PRINCE2, and custom frameworks — PMWeb is configurable to align with your organisation's preferred project management methodology
Discuss your program structure with our team

Feature 02

Estimating and Budget Management

Estimates are not just a starting point — they are the benchmark against which every cost event during delivery is measured. PMWeb's estimating tools allow program managers to build detailed budgets at every level of the WBS, assign contingency, model different funding scenarios, and maintain a complete history of how budgets have evolved from initial feasibility through to approved baseline.

Multi-level budget structures — define estimates from program level down to work package and line item, with roll-up totals at every tier
Contingency and escalation modelling — apply contingency at any level of the WBS and model escalation scenarios for multi-year programs
Funding source tracking — assign multiple funding sources to budgets and track expenditure against each source independently
Budget version history — maintain a full audit trail of budget revisions from feasibility estimate through to construction baseline
Direct connection to cost controls — estimates flow directly into PMWeb's cost management module, eliminating manual re-entry when projects move to construction

Feature 03

Procurement and Vendor Selection

Procurement is one of the highest-risk phases of any capital works program — and one of the most documentation-intensive. PMWeb manages the full procurement process from initial request for tender through evaluation, shortlisting, and contract award, with all documents, communications, and decisions stored in the same system that will manage the resulting contracts throughout construction.

Tender and RFP management — issue tenders, manage addenda, and track respondent status from a single procurement register
Bid evaluation and scoring — configurable scoring matrices allow consistent evaluation of submissions against weighted criteria
Prequalification registers — maintain approved supplier and contractor registers with qualification status, insurance records, and performance history
Seamless contract creation — awarded procurement records convert directly into contracts in PMWeb's contract management module, maintaining a single chain of custody for all documentation
Full audit trail — every decision, communication, and document version is recorded and timestamped, supporting probity requirements for public sector procurement
